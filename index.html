things I believe
Five bets on AI × biology
01
The next breakthrough drugs won't be discovered in wet labs — they'll emerge from AI trained on genomic instability patterns no human researcher could trace across millions of samples.
From my work on Bacteroides genome rearrangements at Sabanci University, Istanbul.
02
Epidemic modeling is about to be transformed — not by better transmission math, but by LLMs that synthesize real-time behavioral data about why people actually move, gather, and comply.
From my hyperbolic network modeling work under ISTA's Simon Rella.
03
Central Asia and Eastern Europe are the most underserved AI markets on earth. Low-resource language models + broken health infrastructure = the highest-impact, most neglected opportunity in global health AI.
I've seen this gap up close building PulmoAI in Kazakhstan.
04
Behavioral AI — models that understand the psychology behind choices, not just the choices themselves — is the missing layer in every mental health and addiction app that has ever failed to retain users.
From my NLP work on 3,000+ longitudinal behavioral observations at Berkeley.
05
The researcher who can pipette and write a training loop is 10× more valuable than either alone. We're not training those people yet — and the gap between wet-lab intuition and computational power is where the biggest discoveries will happen in the next decade.
That's the gap I'm deliberately building toward, one semester at a time.
